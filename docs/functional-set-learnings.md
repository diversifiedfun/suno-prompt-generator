# Functional Music Set Learnings — catalog for the Set Builder

Companion to `suno-prompt-learnings.md` (single-track craft) and `skill/references/dj-set-playbook.md`
(party/dancefloor set craft). This file owns the **functional / wellness** set family and the
**translation layer** that turns any preset into Suno Style/Exclude/Lyrics fields.

Distilled 2026-07-02 from a 3-agent research fan-out (sleep/relaxation/ambient · meditation/healing ·
motivation/focus/uplifting + translation). Full source files were the research drafts; every non-obvious
claim below carries a confidence tier + real source.

## Evidence tiers (extends the house scale)

- **Peer-reviewed** — clinical/RCT literature directly on point (strongest).
- **Official** — help.suno.com or a peer-reviewed source with an unambiguous finding.
- **Confirmed** — 3+ independent community/production sources agree.
- **Anecdotal** — single source, practitioner claim, or genre convention.
- **Contested/Pseudoscience** — widely marketed, but controlled evidence is absent, contradictory,
  or the mechanism isn't physically plausible. Used for "healing frequency" claims.

## Honesty policy (blocking — never violate)

Every preset separates **(a) production convention** — what listeners request and how creators build it
(real, goes into presets) — from **(b) the therapeutic claim** attached to it (evaluated separately).
Never let (b) leak into (a). Sonic labels like "528Hz-tuned drone" or "binaural layer" are legitimate
*production descriptors*; claims of effect ("repairs DNA," "heals your body," "entrains your brain to
theta," "syncs your heartbeat") are **never** surfaced in generated copy, track titles, or preset marketing.
The legitimate base (NIH/NCCIH: music-based interventions have real, evidence-graded benefits on pain,
anxiety, depression — https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/providers/digest/music-and-health-science) does
**not** validate any specific frequency's special claim.

# Family 2 — Functional / wellness (NEW)

Nine presets across three research clusters. **Maturity flag per preset:** `sourced` = grounded in
peer-reviewed or convergent-convention research below; `starter` = reasonable default, revisit with usage
data. **All 8-track energy contours are house-convention interpolations** — the underlying research is
almost entirely single-track/single-session; no study covers multi-track *set* arcs. Treat contours as
starting defaults, not citations.

## Energy scale (1–10, shared with the DJ playbook)
1 = ambient/beatless · 3 = slow, faint pulse · 5 = "people start dancing" · 7 = anthem · 8–9 = festival.
The entire wellness family lives in **1–8**; sleep/meditation/healing never exceed 4.

## Sleep  — maturity: sourced
- **Arc:** descending taper `3 – 3 – 2 – 2 – 2 – 1 – 1 – 1`. Set = one long taper; never flat-line 1.
- **Energy band:** 1–3 (mostly 1–2).
- **BPM:** descends 70–75 → 60–65 → <60/beatless. Core 60–80 BPM sleep-tempo band is **Peer-reviewed**
  (Pan & Wang 2025, *Frontiers in Sleep*, PMC12713922; Feng 2018 meta-analysis, 20 RCTs / 1,339 patients).
- **Vocals:** **none.** Instrumental strictly outperforms vocal in RCTs (lyrics engage language regions,
  fight cognitive disengagement). Wordless vowels only if any, never in the back half. **Peer-reviewed.**
- **Palette:** solo/felt piano, long sustain pedal, slow-attack pads, bowed strings (long held tones, no
  vibrato bite), Rhodes heavily reverbed/half-speed, distant wordless choir pad, tape warmth, room-tone
  bed under the instrument (not lead).
- **Do:** slow, sustained, legato, soft, consonant, low dynamic range, stable volume, long tail, beatless.
- **Don't:** percussion, accented beats, staccato, sudden dynamic swings, dissonance, hooky melody, lyrics,
  spoken word, prominent bass, key changes.
- **⚠ Key decision — minor vs major:** RCTs *disfavor* minor keys for sleep (minor reads as arousal/tension);
  they favor **major/Dorian**. This contradicts the market convention of "sad piano for sleep." **Confirmed.**
- **Length:** clinical dose 30–45 min/session; favor long tracks (chain 4–6 × up-to-8-min rather than 10+
  short cuts) to minimize audible transitions. Set should have a natural stopping point (fade to true
  silence), not loop all night.
- **Set-craft:** seamless is the whole game — no audible track boundaries; never end on a rise; stay in one
  key neighborhood, tightening as it descends.

## Relaxation / Calm  — maturity: sourced
- **Arc:** gentle descent `4 – 4 – 3 – 3 – 3 – 2 – 2 – 1`. Daytime unwind, not sleep-onset — tolerates a
  felt pulse and mild interplay.
- **Energy band:** 2–4.
- **BPM:** 60–90, sweet spot 60–80 (resting-HR entrainment logic). Reference point: Marconi Union
  "Weightless" starts ~60 and drops to ~50; 2017 Mindlab study attributed ~65% anxiety reduction partly to
  that taper (**Confirmed**, industry-commissioned).
- **Vocals:** none-to-wordless; soft breathy vowel or distant choir pad OK. Full lyrics = Don't (strong
  default, not absolute).
- **Palette:** atmospheric pads, spacious reverb tails, single-note wide-spaced piano/guitar, pentatonic/
  modal (Dorian/Lydian) phrases, maj7/min9/sus2/sus4 chords resolving slowly, low-density arp textures,
  sparse rainstick/bowl/wind-chime color, natural dynamics (avoid overcompression).
- **Do:** gentle, sustained, spacious, warm, floating, suspended, consistent palette.
- **Don't:** abrupt transients, harsh plucked attacks as primary texture, triplet rhythms, dissonance,
  dramatic modulation, maximalist layering, loudness spikes.
- **Set-craft (best-sourced principle in the whole family):** balance repetition vs. controlled novelty —
  brains disengage from *fully* predictable OR *fully* unpredictable material; the failure mode is
  *semi-predictable*. Alternate cleanly-looping tracks with slowly-evolving Eno-style ones. Keep dynamic
  range flat within and across tracks. **Entrainment takes ~5 min** → the first track must hold the target
  tempo/dynamic band ≥5 min before the set can "ask" for deeper relaxation. Carry 2–3 signature textures
  set-wide. (signalsounds.com synthesis citing 2017 Mindlab + 2012 *J. Music Therapy* + 1951 sustained-tone
  study — **Medium-High**.)

## Ambient / Soundscape  — maturity: sourced
- **Arc:** near-flat `2 – 2 – 2 – 1 – 2 – 1 – 1 – 1`. Eno's brief: "as ignorable as it is interesting" —
  non-linear plateau is authentic; a *slight* day→night drift gives purpose. **Low** on the numbers.
- **Energy band:** 1–2.
- **BPM:** beatless default; if pulsed, <70 and as texture. Strongest fit = long asynchronous loops of
  deliberately mismatched lengths (16–38s) that never realign (Eno's core technique). **Confirmed** (genre
  convention, not science).
- **Vocals:** none / wordless shapeless drone-vocal only.
- **Palette:** sustained drones (10s+ holds), 3–4 note independent loops phasing in/out, Rhodes/piano
  through echo→slowed/smeared, field recordings (rain/wind/waves/birdsong) as a *continuous bed*,
  pink-noise-adjacent "shushing," brown-noise low rumble, sparse spaced fragments over continuous melody.
  Pink/brown noise sleep/relax association = **Peer-reviewed / Medium** (Sleep Foundation, cites NLM studies
  but flags "more studies needed").
- **Do:** drone, sustained, evolving, generative, phasing, textural, spacious, continuous, seamless.
- **Don't:** melody-forward writing, hooks, verse/chorus, percussion (unless "ambient techno," <70 & minimal),
  harsh highs, **audible loop seams** (worst failure mode here), anything that reads as a "song" not an
  "environment."
- **Length:** longest of any category (Eno tracks 6–17 min; loops need time to phase). Favor max generation
  length + explicit long-bar-count outro directives.
- **Set-craft:** non-repeating combinatorics; nature layers = continuous bed, cross-faded, never hard-cut
  "forest → ocean"; loop-seam invisibility matters more here than anywhere.

## Meditation (mindfulness / breath)  — maturity: sourced
- **Arc:** flat `2 – 2 – 3 – 3 – 3 – 2 – 2 – 1`. No climax is the point. Slight settling-in dip, slight
  return-to-alertness taper.
- **Energy band:** 1–3.
- **BPM = two separate numbers:** (i) *musical bed* — beatless or ~50–70 equiv; **prefer "beatless" + explicit
  percussion exclusion — stating a BPM invites a beat**; (ii) *breath-pacing cue* (separate axis, guided
  tracks only) — **4–6 breaths/min** (e.g. 5 in/1 hold/5 out/1.5 hold ≈ 5 bpm). Breath physiology is
  **Confirmed/Peer-reviewed** (Lin 2014 HRV; PMC10741869 review) — a real effect of the *breathing*, not the
  music. The "60 BPM syncs your heart rate" claim is **Contested/Pseudoscience** as stated.
- **Vocals:** none/wordless base; soft spoken guide = named alternate; single-word mantra (om, so-hum) as
  texture. No lyrical songwriting.
- **Palette:** sustained drone/pad (analog pad, bowed strings, shruti box/harmonium), Tibetan/crystal bowls,
  wordless hum/"ahh" pad, distant chimes, minimal/no percussion, long reverb tails, slow filter sweeps,
  occasional flute/ney, very sparse nature layer (busier bed competes with breath focus).
- **Set-craft:** sequencing follows the breath, not a musical arc — any change over 30s+, never on a downbeat;
  hold breath ratio constant per track, change only between tracks; closing track *sheds* texture, no bright
  "wake-up" sting.

## Sound Healing (solfeggio / bowls / gongs)  — maturity: sourced (production) / see caveats (claims)
- **Arc:** flattest, `1 – 1 – 2 – 2 – 2 – 2 – 1 – 1`. Often a **"menu"** of frequency/instrument-labeled
  modules, not a narrative.
- **Energy band:** 1–2. Beatless is the strong norm.
- **BPM:** beatless; pace by strike/decay ("bowl struck every 15–20s, long decay"), not tempo. Frequency-
  labeling (a drone whose *fundamental* is tuned to e.g. 528Hz) is a **real, concrete production choice** —
  it's just retuning a drone, legitimate sonically regardless of the claim attached.
- **Vocals:** none / wordless overtone hum / single mantra syllable.
- **Palette:** singing bowls (rubbed sustain or struck long-decay), gongs, tuning forks, bells/chimes,
  frequency-tuned drone pad, soft overtone chant, optional binaural stereo-tone layer, minimal nature,
  long natural reverb, one drone/chord held for minutes.
- **⚠ Evidence caveats (surface honestly; never as fact):**
  - **528Hz "repairs DNA"/"Love Frequency"** — Contested/Pseudoscience; no credible controlled evidence
    (howstuffworks science-explainer: "scarce," "unverified"). Advocacy sites cite single non-peer-reviewed
    postings.
  - **Solfeggio "ancient origin"** — Contested; a 20th-c. construction layered onto the old ut-re-mi solfège
    naming. Don't reproduce as history.
  - **Binaural beats "entrain your brain"** — genuinely **mixed**: 2023 systematic review (14 studies:
    5 support / 8 contradict / 1 mixed, PMC10198548); 2025 study (n=65) found calming across theta/alpha/beta
    with *no* frequency-specific difference (PMC12145584). Use "may support relaxation in some listeners,"
    never "entrains to theta."
  - **Singing-bowl mood benefit** — real but limited: UC San Diego n=62, **no control group** (PMC5871151) —
    shows "guided calm listening helps," not that bowls specifically are the active ingredient.
  - **432Hz** — one small pilot in a narrow population; effect (if any) is tempo/dynamics/instrumentation, not
    the tuning. Safe as a preset *name/flavor*, not a scientific claim.

## Yoga / Spa / Wellness background  — maturity: sourced
- **Arc:** mild practice shape `2 – 3 – 3 – 4 – 4 – 3 – 3 – 2` (arrival → flow/asana → cool-down/savasana).
  The one wellness category where a gentle mid-set rise is expected. Never "danceable."
- **Energy band:** 2–4 (highest wellness ceiling).
- **BPM:** ~60–90 where pulsed (soft non-percussive pulse or frame-drum feel in "flow" tracks); beatless
  bookends. Practice-pacing convention, no health claim.
- **Vocals:** wordless dominant (hums, "ahh," occasional wordless float); soft spoken cueing = alternate;
  Sanskrit-adjacent vocal *tone* as color, not lyric.
- **Palette:** warm analog pads, soft acoustic guitar/kalimba arps, shruti box/harmonium, gentle frame drum/
  hand perc (soft, non-driving), flute/ney/duduk lines, prominent nature layers (more than meditation),
  bowls as accent, long filtered pad swells, gentle reverb wash.
- **Set-craft:** mild arc is desirable but the mid "peak" rises in *warmth/gentle presence*, not intensity
  spikes (no drops/builds); seamless crossfades; savasana tracks shed rhythm → melody → bare drone.

## Motivation / Hype / Workout (non-party)  — maturity: sourced (tempo) / starter (arc, vocal form)
- **Arc:** ascend-then-hold `3 – 5 – 6 – 7 – 8 – 8 – 8 – 6`. Climb spans tracks 1–4 (don't compress into
  track 1), 3-track peak plateau (the grind), single step down finisher (not a hard stop).
- **Energy band:** 5–8 (never beatless; never 9–10 festival — this is solo psych-up, not a crowd peak).
- **BPM:** 120–160, sweet spot 128–150 (warm-up 120–140, high-intensity 140–160, cooldown 110–120).
  **High confidence** — Karageorghis 125 BPM = +15% endurance vs. silence (center4research summary);
  Thakare 2017 (PMC5435671); kingsbox + outdoor-fit BPM tables converge.
- **Vocals:** pick one per track — (1) instrumental driving beat, or (2) short repeated **chant/hype**
  (shouted gang-vocal affirmation/ad-lib), NOT narrative lyrics.
- **Palette:** punchy kick, driving/rolling bass, syncopated hats/trap snare, sparing riser/brass stab
  (not a literal EDM drop), distorted power chords (rock variant), gang-chant/shout layer, handclap/stomp.
- **Do:** driving, relentless, pounding, surging, propulsive, gritty, gang-vocal chant. **Don't:** gentle,
  mellow, dreamy, sleepy, lounge, smooth (energy-killer list) + empty evaluative words (epic, powerful).
- **Set-craft:** vary vocal FORM + signature lead per track (808 vs distorted guitar vs brass vs trap hats);
  peak holds, doesn't spike-and-crash; end on a step-down finisher.

## Deep Focus / Concentration (instrumental)  — maturity: sourced
- **Arc:** plateau `4 – 5 – 5 – 5 – 5 – 5 – 5 – 4`, near-zero variation; soft ease-in/out bookends.
- **Energy band:** 3–5 (never "danceable" — foot-tapping pulls attention off the task).
- **BPM:** 60–90 (lo-fi convention). **Medium** — the peer-reviewed study used lo-fi as its instrumental
  condition; supports the genre choice, not a specific BPM.
- **Vocals — HARD RULE:** instrumental or wordless/non-lexical only. **No intelligible lyrics.** **High
  confidence** — Souza & Barbosa 2023 (*J. Cognition*, PMC10162369): lyrics hurt verbal memory, visual
  memory, reading comprehension (d≈−0.3); instrumental lo-fi = no credible effect. Native-language lyrics
  worst (Sun 2024), so "no intelligible lyrics" is the safe default.
- **Palette:** warm filtered Rhodes/piano loop, soft vinyl crackle/tape hiss, sustained pad, muted lo-fi
  drums (soft kick, brushed hats), minimal filtered sub, one or two repeating motifs (not evolving).
  Brain.fm design principle: "attention-grabbing elements subdued or removed" (**Medium**, company source
  citing its own Woods 2024 *Communications Biology*).
- **Do:** steady, sustained, filtered, understated, looping, muted, warm. **Don't:** sudden, drop, scream,
  chant, hook, big swell, key-change, crash, spoken word, ad-libs, any lyric.
- **Structure:** **ban `[Chorus]`/`[Drop]`/`[Hook]`** — use `[Instrumental]`/`[Interlude]` looping cues.
- **Set-craft:** ±0–1 energy steps (plateau, not arc — opposite of DJ discipline, deliberately); rotate lead
  texture per track (piano → guitar → pad → mallet), vary sound-world not intensity; avoid same lead
  back-to-back; order matters less than in a DJ set.

## Uplifting / Positive-Mood  — maturity: starter
- **Arc:** one clear anthemic peak `4 – 5 – 6 – 7 – 7 – 6 – 6 – 5` (build once ~track 4–5, land warm).
- **Energy band:** 4–7 (bright, buoyant; stops short of festival/aggressive).
- **BPM:** 100–128 (upbeat-pop convention). **Low/convention.**
- **Vocals:** full lead vocal, bright major-key delivery, layered/group-vocal lift on choruses — the one
  functional category where the standard 3-layer vocal spec applies unchanged, delivery biased warm/open/
  soaring. Real positive-message lyrics welcome.
- **Palette:** bright acoustic/clean electric strums, major-key piano, warm horn stabs, handclaps,
  tambourine, wide shimmering pads, gang/group backing vocals. **Mode (major vs minor) is the highest-leverage
  positivity lever** — **Medium** (2024 major-minor-mode review, ScienceDirect S1571064524001672; Spotify
  "valence" construct, PMC9522267 — note the API endpoint was deprecated late 2025, construct only).
- **Do:** bright, warm, buoyant, radiant, open, soaring (+ concrete instrument), major-key. **Don't:**
  minor-key gloom, distorted/harsh, industrial, monotone, brooding + empty evaluative words.
- **Set-craft:** one anthemic peak (not a plateau, not a grind); vary tempo ±4–8 BPM + lead instrument while
  holding major-key brightness; landing track resolves on a sustained major chord/gentle fade.

# Translation layer — preset → Suno fields

Reuses the existing `generator.js` SYSTEM_PROMPT rules + `knowledge.js` vocabulary. What changes:

### (a) Style field template
`[Genre/subgenre] + [BPM or "beatless, no fixed tempo"] + [energy-band adjective] + [2–4 palette
descriptors] + [vocal clause] + [optional set-position cue]`, front-loaded, ~150–180 chars (tighter than
the single-track 200 to leave headroom for per-track lead-texture swaps).

### (b) Exclude field
Category Don't-words → match `GENRE_TRAPS` on the landed genre → cap 2/track, **rotate which 2 per track**
so Exclude itself adds per-track distinctness. **New `GENRE_TRAPS` entries needed:**
- `Focus ambient / Lo-fi instrumental` → cliché rain/ocean/white-noise SFX → `"no rain/ocean sfx, sustained
  pad texture instead"` (the single most likely default-trap in the family).
- `Uplifting pop` → saccharine final-chorus key-change → `"no key-change modulation, sustain the groove"`.
- `Motivational trap` → aggressive/explicit ad-libs → `"gym-crowd chant energy, no explicit aggression"`
  (a chant-permitting variant of the existing narrative-rap "no ad-libs" fix).
- **Deep-focus set-wide exclusion** (every track, on top of genre-trap matching): `"no sudden dynamic shifts,
  no spoken word"`.

### (c) New `CONTRADICTIONS` pairs
`["beatless","BPM"]`, `["instrumental","vocal hook"]`, `["steady","anthemic build"]`,
`["low-distraction","big dynamic swell"]`.

### (d) Rules that need a functional exception (vs. single-track SYSTEM_PROMPT)
- **Rule 4 (mandatory BPM + 3-layer vocal spec):** Deep-focus is instrumental/wordless *by default* (skip the
  vocal spec entirely, not as fallback); beatless categories (meditation/healing/ambient/sleep back-half) skip
  BPM → `"beatless, no fixed tempo"`.
- **Structure scaffold** is category-conditioned, not the one hardcoded scaffold: Deep-focus bans
  `[Chorus]/[Drop]/[Hook]`; Motivation front-loads `[Pre-Chorus]/[Chorus]`; Uplifting/Sleep/etc. use their own.
- Rules 1,2,3,6,7 apply unchanged across all categories. Rule 10 target tightens to ~150–180 chars.

### (e) Per-track brief shape (Phase 1 = plan output)
`{ trackIndex, arcEnergy, bpmOrBeatless, vocalDefault, leadTexture, doWords[], dontWords[], structureHint }`
— `leadTexture` must differ from the previous track; `vocalDefault` is hard-set for deep-focus (never left to
genre convention, which sneaks lyrics into "lo-fi").

# Open decisions for Molly (surfaced in brainstorm)

1. **Sleep key default:** evidence (major/Dorian) vs. market convention (minor "sad piano"). Recommend
   defaulting to evidence + making it a differentiator ("built on sleep-science, not just vibes").
2. **Energy contours** are house-convention interpolations, not literature-sourced — ship as defaults,
   revisit with usage data.
3. **Healing-frequency presets** ship as production *flavors* (name/tuning), never with medical claims.
4. **Maturity labels** (`sourced`/`starter`) are honest signals in the UI, and graduate as real sets get built.
